The thing most authors don’t realise

When you publish on Amazon you choose up to three categories. But Amazon doesn’t simply take your word for it — its system reads your title, subtitle, blurb and keywords and decides whether your book really belongs there.

If those signals say “generic contemporary romance” but you chose “Clean & Wholesome,” Amazon can quietly file you in the broad category instead — the one with a hundred thousand books and none of your specific readers. And once the book is live, that placement is painful to undo.

So what exactly is a “BISAC” code?

BISAC (Book Industry Standards and Communications) is the publishing industry’s official list of subject codes — a standard way of saying precisely what a book is. Every romance sub-type has one:

FIC027270  =  Romance / Clean & Wholesome

FIC027560  =  Romance / Dark Romance

FIC027070  =  Romance / Historical / Regency

Think of a BISAC code as your book’s shelf label — written in a language every bookstore, library and retailer understands.

Here’s the part that trips authors up: on Amazon you no longer pick a BISAC code yourself. Since 2023 you choose an Amazon category, and Amazon works out the matching BISAC automatically, behind the scenes. So the category you pick is your BISAC — you just never see the code. It still matters directly if you go “wide” (selling through IngramSpark, libraries and other retailers), where the BISAC is exactly how your book gets shelved.

Why this matters to you: the category you pick — and the BISAC it maps to — is the single biggest lever controlling where your book appears. Your keywords, subtitle and blurb then either back it up (so Amazon keeps you there and readers find you) or fight it (so you drift).
